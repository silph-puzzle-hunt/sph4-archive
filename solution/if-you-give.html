Solution: If You Give...
Answer: SWALLOWS

Written by mstang

This puzzle consists of a sequence of clues that describe various situations, specifically the consequences of "giving" somebody something: what they might do with what you give them, how they might react, et cetera. These descriptions are reminiscent of the If You Give... children's book series by Laura Numeroff and Felicia Bond, whose first and most famous entry is If You Give a Mouse a Cookie.

Like in the titles of those books, each clue can be filled in with two words in a way that logically completes the phrase "If you give (a/your) SOMETHING A SOMETHING." The key observation is that the last three words of each phrase form a new word: for example, the first clue solves as "If you give your UNCLE A REST," with the corresponding word UNCLEAREST.

Reading these letters in order gives the cluephrase EATS OR SONGBIRDS EIGHT. An eight-letter word matching both "eats" and "songbirds" is SWALLOWS, the answer.

Author’s Notes

The idea for this puzzle came about randomly when I noticed that the word "catacomb" could be read as "cat a comb." This in turn reminded me of the If You Give a Mouse a Cookie series of books and led me to imagine a cat being given a comb to comb its own fur. As you can see, it turns out that there are a reasonably large number of words that can be read this way!

I think that the extraction step could have been more elegant. For one, I could have ditched the extraction line at the bottom of the puzzle and just given the letters A-T out of order in the clues, so that solvers would sort the extracted letters themselves. I also could have found a way to use the words UNCLEAREST, TOMAHAWK, etc. in extraction, so that the extraction step also relied on the fact that you can form these words. But in the end, this puzzle was based on a simple idea and didn't need to be more complicated. Overall, I'm happy with how it turned out.
