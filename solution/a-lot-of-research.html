Each pair of a gray bar with red regions and image/diagram is a description of some part of an Unraveled video, the Polygon series hosted by Brian David Gilbert. After deciphering the diagrams, one Pokémon can be extracted from each video. The Pokémon are in alphabetical order, suggesting they need to be reordered.

The context in which each Pokémon is mentioned in the Perfect Pokérap (which is one of the videos in which BDG mentions the title phrase, although it is ultimately from the Game of the Year video after some paraphrasing) corresponds to one of the final red grids, with the above Pokémon corresponding to the asterisked box. For each other box with a number, indexing the number into that box's corresponding Pokémon or word produces the cluephrase, "LIKE POKEPARK MARACAS".

The item in Poképark with "maracas" in the name is called “Groovy Maracas”. The answer is GROOVY.

Nothing makes me want to write a puzzle about something as much as hearing that you shouldn’t or can’t write a puzzle about something (sometimes entirely sensibly, so you have to subvert the actual reason it’s a bad idea). This has been the case since the first puzzle I published in a major puzzlehunt in 2015.

The current puzzle probably didn’t come out as well as the average puzzle I write this way, and more than likely falls into the trap whereby, if I’m trying to convince you that something is interesting by making you do a lot of painstaking research about it, it’s more likely to backfire than work. But I couldn’t not write a puzzle about the subject. Anyway, this is me saying that you should watch the Unraveled videos, and also like all of BDG’s work since then, and I hope it didn’t backfire too much.

For the longest time, the puzzle was titled “Gesamtkunstwerk”, and linking it with Unraveled/BDG likely required more familiarity or googling it with the word “Pokemon”. We were extremely amused that the 2021 Teammate Hunt used the word in their wrapup seemingly out of nowhere. Unfortunately we nerfed it because some testsolvers got stuck on looking at only the Pokérap.

I apologize for the very large number of errata in this puzzle, but you can probably imagine why.
