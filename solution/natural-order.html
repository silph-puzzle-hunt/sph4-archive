Solution: Natural Order
Answer: FALSE POSITIVE

Written by Level 51

This is a standard crossword, so the natural approach is to start filling it out. As solvers do so, they'll realize that (perhaps surprisingly!) there are no tricks to the grid fill and the entries go in completely normally, as shown in the grid below.

However, as solvers fill the grid, a few clues might stand out, which seem to hint at the theme and structure of this puzzle:

25-Across references Pallet Town, from the Pokémon series of games, which implies that this puzzle is about Pokémon somehow. 135-Across indicates that "Order" in the puzzle's title isn't hintful, implying a thematic link to "Natural" or "Nature" somehow, and 1-Down suggests that the puzzle involves a chart of some kind. A little searching of "Pokémon", "chart", and either "natural" or "nature" turns up a Pokémon nature chart, which indeed is 5-by-5 and, in its most commonly represented form, has increases on the rows and decreases across the columns. We can line up a standard Pokémon nature chart with our central 5-by-5 block to correspond each nature to a letter.

After this, solvers might note that some natures in the chart seem familiar; in fact, 10 of them appear as Across clues in the crossword! Since each nature corresponds to one letter in the 5-by-5 block, we can take the 10 corresponding letters and arrange them in the order the clues appeared in the crossword.

Author’s Notes

While many solvers are aware of common Pokémon datasets, I wanted to touch on some less-known aspects of the game. I like crosswords where the structure of the grid has some meaning, so this was the natural (ha) outcome of my brainstorming. At this point in time, I had been doing the NYT crossword on a daily basis, and wanted to take a proper crack at constructing a grid with a sizeable number of theme answers and a clean fill. All in all, the grid took about five days to fill in a manner I found satisfactory, which has definitely dissuaded me from ever attempting to write anything to submit to the NYT.

I hope people made jokes in their team chats about the clues to 75-Down and 76-Down.
