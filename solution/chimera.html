Solution: Chimera
Answer: TELESCOPE

Written by lovemathboy

This puzzle contains 4 different grids of logic puzzles, each split as a left, middle, and right fragment. As the title of the puzzle suggests, solvers should take a left, middle, and right fragment from different genres and combine them to form a mash-up loop puzzle. The type of dotted line on the left and right of fragments indicates that every assembled grid should contain 3 fragments. The unique way to make all 4 grids such that every grid has a solution is:

Extracted word from the loop: ASTRONOMICAL. Unused letters: INITIALLY.

Extracted word from the loop: OPTICAL. Unused letters: SECONDLY.

Extracted word from the loop: INSTRUMENT. Unused letters: THIRDLY.

Extracted word from the loop: NINE LETTERS. Unused letters: FINALLY.

The final clue phrase is ASTRONOMICAL OPTICAL INSTRUMENT NINE LETTERS, which clues the word TELESCOPE.

Author’s Notes

Although you can solve this puzzle assuming each grid has a unique answer (using uniqueness deductions) and each grid contains different genres, the puzzle was written without that in mind. Hence, you can arrive at the same unique answer by just assuming you need to assemble 4 grids using any left, any middle, and any right such that each grid has at least 1 solution.

As a fun fact, the Balance Loop puzzle initially only had 4 number clues. However, the puzzle was deemed too hard and Balance Loop was nerfed to containing 12 number clues.
