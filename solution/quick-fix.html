This puzzle is about interpreting prefixes and suffixes "creatively" (or, as it's known in the real world, incorrectly). Each clue on the left clues a word via a root word and two in some combination of prefixes and suffixes, as shown below. Here, we denote the root word in uppercase, and the two prefixes or suffixes in lowercase.

Performing the described indexing yields the clue phrase MORE MORE MAGIC STICK. Together with the (8) enumeration on the puzzle page, we can determine that this is cluing one last clue; "more" quite naturally leads to the -er suffix, and we can conclude that the answer to this puzzle is WANDERER.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle was inspired by a dream, sort of. I woke up one morning with the distinct association of DEVOTEE with the definition of "one who loses votes" in my mind, put it in my ideas spreadsheet, and went back to sleep. Later, I noticed WANDERER in the answers, and noted the idea as a definite possibility, though I was concerned that the idea might not be interesting enough for the hunt. In the end, I was won over by our need for more puzzles, but also because I thought MORE MORE MAGIC STICK was a really funny extracted phrase. Hopefully my choices of clues were enough to make the puzzle sufficiently interesting!

(Also, yes, the first clue is a deliberate reference to that particular Key and Peele sketch.)
