Written by Level 51

This puzzle contains a number of Tweets commenting on the NYT Spelling Bee, a daily puzzle where solvers are given six outer letters and one central letter and have to construct as many words as possible from this set such that the central letter is always included. Here, each Tweet allows us to reconstruct a single Spelling Bee puzzle. Solvers should use the seven-distinct-letter constraints of the puzzles in order to solve some otherwise possibly ambiguous clues (for example, the third Tweet's "programming language" and "ice cream flavor"). To aid further in solving, the three words besides the perfect pangram are clued in alphabetical order in each Tweet.

The solutions to each Tweet are as follows. In each case, the three additional words (besides the perfect pangram) allow us to determine what the puzzle's central letter was. We can extract those central letters, in the given order of the Tweets, to spell out the puzzle answer NEVER MIND.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle was inspired by @srahman2020 on Twitter. I cannot remember how it happened, but his daily Spelling Bee review somehow made its way onto my Twitter feed, I had some fun attempting to back-construct the puzzle using his comments, and then I realized it would be a decent idea for a puzzle. The initial draft of this puzzle involved much harder clues, but since the answer had a letter pattern that made it pretty easy to guess from a few letters, we decided to dial it back so there'd be less incentive to skip large swaths of the puzzle and it'd be more fun for everyone.
