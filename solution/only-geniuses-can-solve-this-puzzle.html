Solution: Only Geniuses Can Solve This Puzzle!!
Answer: SNAKE EGGPLANT

Written by mstang

This puzzle consists of a series of images containing text, emoji, and blanks. A first step is to notice that the top of each image features a sentence of the form "Only XX.XX% of people can solve this!" Taking the numbers before and after the decimal point in each image and converting them to letters via A=1, ..., Z=26 yields CLUE THREE TEN ANS FIVE EIGHT. This tells us that we ought to receive a cluephrase enumerated (3 10), which leads to an answer enumerated (5 8).

Turning to the main content of the images, solvers hopefully realize that each depicts a famous mathematical problem, theorem, or conjecture, with some key words replaced by emoji. (These are in fact a tasteful pastiche parody of the "fruit math" meme.) Note that the images are sorted alphabetically by their contents. The goal is to identify what each image is referencing (either by clever searching or by using prior mathematical knowledge) and fill in the blanks corresponding to each emoji. As a small bit of confirmation, the text of each image ends with either "!!", if the problem has been solved, or "??", if the problem is unsolved (or unsolvable).

Author’s Notes

Would you believe this puzzle was written in two days in the week prior to the hunt?

As the deadline was approaching, Level and I were talking one day about puzzle ideas that might fit this answer. At some point I brought up that the snake eggplant is a "cultivar" of the eggplant, and my 2am brain leapt to "Hey, let's do a puzzle about multivariable calculus where all the variables are replaced with fruits and vegetables, and call it Cultivariable Calculus!" When this was justifiably shot down by multiple people, Level instead mentioned the "fruit math" meme as another way to incorporate both produce and math, and so we were off to the races.

Although it was rushed, leading to a small erratum involving an extra blank, I'm glad that this puzzle came out pretty solid. I wasn't actually able to find many famous-ish mathematical problems/theorems/conjectures that can be emoji-fied in a fair way; you're seeing almost all the ones I felt could be used. So I'm also happy that I was able to extract the letters I needed in a reasonable way. Several mathy puzzlers told me that they enjoyed/got a good laugh out of this puzzle, and for that I am very grateful.
