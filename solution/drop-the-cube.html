Author’s Notes

This was a very fun puzzle to construct, as I've always been interested by Rubik's Cubes (even though I never got around to learning how to solve one, even after writing this), and writing this had me researching quite a bit on how they work and what not. Going in, I already knew that I couldn't just slap 9 stickers of each color around and call it a day, as there are eight unique corners and twelve unique edges, and the central pieces are unchangeable. On top of that, some arrangements of the pieces are never fully solvable (which would be a pretty nice prank to just swap one piece on a friend's Rubik's cube, and see how long it takes them to realize it's impossible...). If you're interested in learning more, Wikipedia puts it better than I ever could.

Constructing this puzzle was mostly getting the cube aspect to work, as the words and letters themselves didn't pose much of a problem. During testsolving, this puzzle turned out to be a complete grind-fest as there are a lot of these cubes to solve, and we were mostly testsolving solo or in pairs, which made the bigger puzzles very hard to get through. So I hope you had 5 or so active teammates helping you while you were solving this, or that you were really dedicated to solving this. But regardless:
