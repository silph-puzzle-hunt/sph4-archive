Solution: Straight From The Headlines
Answer: VOLUNTEER CAMPAIGNS

Written by Deusovi

This puzzle is based off of "crash blossoms": headlines that are ambiguous due to the writing style that newspaper headlines often use. The diagrams presented are Reed-Kellogg diagrams.

The given text is in blue, and the situation it describes is fairly ridiculous—it continues the "unintended" reading of the headline. The diagram also has the parsing of the "unintended" reading in blue, and the "intended" one in red.

The headlines are:

UNIVERSITY HOLDS SEXUAL ORIENTATION WEBINAR
("A university held a webinar about sexual orientation." / "A university held a webinar for orientation that turned sexual.")

BAN ON PUBLIC NUDITY IN CONGRESS
("There is a ban on public nudity, currently being discussed in Congress." / "There is a ban on 'being publicly nude in Congress'.")

SHARK ATTACKS PUZZLE EXPERTS
("Experts are confused by shark attacks." / "A shark has attacked experts on puzzles.")

EVIDENCE FOUND OF ANCIENT MAN EATING FISH
("We have discovered a record of someone eating fish." / We have discovered evidence of a man-eating fish.")

COMMUNITY RAISES FUNDS TO HELP DOG BITE VICTIMS
("A community raises funds in order to help victims of dog bites." / "A community raises funds in order to help a dog to bite the victims [of an unstated other event].)

BILL GATES OFF ALZHEIMER'S MEDICATION
("A new bill makes it harder to access medication for Alzheimer's." / "Billionaire Bill Gates is no longer taking medication for Alzheimer's.")

Use the numbers in each diagram to index, and read left to right, to get the answer: VOLUNTEER CAMPAIGNS.
